<p><p>Benchtop laboratory centrifuges are classified into two main types based on their speed capabilities: high speed and low speed models. High-speed centrifuges are designed to operate at much faster speeds, typically ranging from 15,000 to 30,000 RPM, allowing for quick separation of particles in a short amount of time. On the other hand, low-speed centrifuges have slower speeds ranging from 1,000 to 4,000 RPM, making them ideal for gentle separations such as blood components or urine samples without damaging the sample.</p></p>
