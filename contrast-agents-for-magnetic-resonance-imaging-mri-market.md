<p><ul><li>Barium-based Contrast Media</li><li>Iodinated Contrast Media</li><li>Gadolinium-based Contrast Media</li><li>Microbubble Contrast Media</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Contrast agents for MRI are substances used to enhance the visibility of tissues and structures during imaging. Barium-based contrast media are commonly used for gastrointestinal imaging, while iodinated contrast media are suitable for blood vessel visualization. Gadolinium-based contrast media offer excellent tissue enhancement and are often used for brain and spine imaging. Microbubble contrast media are used for enhancing ultrasound imaging of organs such as the heart. Each type of contrast agent has specific advantages and applications in the field of medical imaging.</p></p>
