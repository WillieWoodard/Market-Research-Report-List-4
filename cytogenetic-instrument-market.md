<p><p>Cytogenetic instruments are used for analyzing genetic abnormalities in cells. Types of cytogenetic instruments include Fluorescence in situ Hybridization (FISH), Comparative Genomic Hybridization (CGH), Flow Cytometry, and Multiplex-FISH. FISH is used to detect specific DNA sequences in chromosomes, while CGH is used to identify chromosomal imbalances. Flow Cytometry analyzes DNA content in cells, and Multiplex-FISH allows for the simultaneous detection of multiple DNA sequences. These instruments play a crucial role in genetic research and diagnostics.</p></p>
