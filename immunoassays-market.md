<p><p>Immunoassays are diagnostic techniques that measure the concentration of specific substances in a sample using antibodies. The five main types of immunoassays include Enzyme Immunoassay, Fluorescence Immunoassay, Chemiluminescence Immunoassay, Radioimmunoassay, and Nephelometric Immunoassay. Each type utilizes different detection methods such as enzymes, fluorescence, chemiluminescence, radioisotopes, or light scattering to quantify analytes. These immunoassays are widely used in clinical diagnostics, drug discovery, and research settings due to their high sensitivity and specificity.</p></p>
