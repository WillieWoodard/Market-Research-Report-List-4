<p><p>Modular construction is a method of building in which individual modules or sections are prefabricated in a factory setting, then transported and assembled on-site to create a complete structure. This approach offers benefits such as faster construction times, lower costs, and reduced environmental impact compared to traditional construction methods.</p><p>The modular construction market is experiencing significant growth, driven by factors such as the increasing demand for affordable housing, the need for rapid construction in urban areas, and the focus on sustainability in the construction industry. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.60% during the forecast period.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the modular construction market is the adoption of advanced technologies such as Building Information Modeling (BIM) and modular design software, which help streamline the design and manufacturing process. Additionally, there is a growing interest in modular construction among developers and contractors looking to mitigate project risks and expedite project delivery.</p><p>Overall, the modular construction market is poised for continued growth as more industries recognize the benefits of this innovative construction method.</p></p>

<p><p>Modular construction market types can be broken down into four categories: walls, roof & floors, columns & beams, and other components. Walls refer to pre-fabricated panels that can be easily assembled onsite. Roof & floors modules include pre-made sections that can be quickly installed. Columns & beams are structural components that provide support for the building. Other market types may include specialty modules such as stairs, ramps, or balconies. Each of these market types cater to different aspects of modular construction, making the process more efficient and cost-effective.</p></p>
