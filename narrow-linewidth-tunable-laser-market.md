<p><p>Semiconductor lasers are compact and versatile, offering narrow linewidth tunability. Distributed Feedback Laser Diodes (DFB lasers) have a grating structure that provides single longitudinal mode output. Distributed Bragg Reflector Lasers (DBR lasers) have separate gain and tuning sections, allowing for precise control of the emission wavelength. Other types of narrow linewidth tunable lasers include External Cavity Lasers and Vertical Cavity Surface Emitting Lasers (VCSELs), each with unique characteristics suited for specific applications in telecommunications, spectroscopy, and sensing.</p></p>
