<p><p>Novel Oral Anticoagulants (NOACs) are a type of medication used to prevent blood clots. There are two main types of NOACs: Direct Thrombin Inhibitors and Direct Factor Xa Inhibitors. Direct Thrombin Inhibitors work by directly blocking the action of thrombin, a crucial enzyme in the blood clotting process. Direct Factor Xa Inhibitors, on the other hand, target factor Xa, another key enzyme involved in blood clot formation. Both types of NOACs are commonly used as alternatives to traditional blood thinners like warfarin.</p></p>
