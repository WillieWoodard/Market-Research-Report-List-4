<p><p>Partial Knee Arthroplasty is a surgical procedure used to treat specific areas of the knee affected by arthritis. There are two main types of implants used in this procedure: Fixed Bearing and Mobile Bearing. Fixed Bearing implants have a stationary polyethylene component, providing stability and durability. On the other hand, Mobile Bearing implants allow for some degree of movement within the knee, potentially reducing wear and tear on the implant and providing a more natural feel. Both types aim to improve function and reduce pain in patients with partial knee arthritis.</p></p>
