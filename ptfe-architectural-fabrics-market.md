<p><p>PTFE architectural fabrics are used for a variety of applications, including porous and non-porous fabrics. Porous PTFE fabric allows for air and light transmission while providing weather protection. It is commonly used in canopies and facades. On the other hand, non-porous PTFE fabric is impermeable to air and water, making it ideal for roofing and tension structures. Both types offer durability, flexibility, and high heat resistance, making them popular choices in the architectural industry.</p></p>
