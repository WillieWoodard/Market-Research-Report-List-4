<p><ul><li>Negative Pressure Isolator</li><li>Positive Pressure Isolator</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Rigid isolators are used in various industries to create a controlled environment for handling hazardous materials or conducting experiments. Negative pressure isolators maintain a vacuum inside the enclosure to prevent contaminants from escaping, while positive pressure isolators keep contaminants from entering by maintaining a higher pressure inside. Both types of isolators are essential for ensuring worker safety and protecting the integrity of sensitive processes. Positive pressure isolators are typically used in cleanroom environments, while negative pressure isolators are common in pharmaceutical and medical research settings.</p></p>
